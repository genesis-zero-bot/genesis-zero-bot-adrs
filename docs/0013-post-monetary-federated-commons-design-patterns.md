# Post-Monetary Federated Commons Design Patterns

## Status
Accepted

## Context

Peter Joseph's Integral project and the Common Ground (Teheno) proposal describe a federated post-monetary cooperative economy. These projects offer concrete design patterns for regenerative neighbourhoods and Integral Collective nodes. This ADR captures the structural decisions and their implications.

## Decisions

### D1: Non-Transferable Time Credit Reputation Ledger

Labor reciprocity is anchored in a time credit mechanism. It functions not as a currency but as a non-transferable reputation-based ledger of contribution. The value of contribution is not derived from what others are willing to give in return, but how the cooperative or federation values and integrates that work into the whole.

### D2: Open-Source Design System with AI Feedback Constraints

All design structures are open for contribution guided by sustainability and efficiency parameters. An open-access AI-assisted crowdsourced design CAD-type platform incorporates efficiency constraints including environmental constraints. AI feedback subsystems guide design optimisation. Labor valuation is dynamically weighted through feedback reflecting real-time demand, skill specificity, and sustainability priorities.

### D3: Federated Node Architecture

Each cooperative is autonomous but connected with the rest of the broader network through shared protocols, open-source design, and efficiency constraints. Integration with the reputation-based ledger of contribution translates labor into reciprocal support for the total network. The network of co-ops functions as a single entity with many organs.

### D4: Government University as Political Feasibility Anchor

Common Ground proposes establishing autonomous communities anchored by public universities. This leverages existing government investment infrastructure (land grant universities in Brazil, 10 new ones announced in 2024 alone), administrative autonomy, tax-exempt status, and non-profit structure. The anchor institution provides a politically feasible entry point that requires no upfront administrative reform.

### D5: Horizontal Governance with Temporary Practical Leadership

Fully democratic and horizontal governance. No hierarchies other than temporary positions of leadership necessary for processes of practical nature and for dialogue with external institutions. Competition has no technical place whatsoever. The organisational intention is to eliminate the need for third-party intervention or management as much as possible.

### D6: Sustainability Constraints as Structural Codification

Ecological sustainability is not an incentive or a policy — it is codified into the structure of the system. The regulatory design embeds environmental constraints as parameters that cannot be overridden by preference. This mirrors Ostrom's eighth principle: embedded in the rules of the system itself.

### D7: Transitional Hybrid Interface Mechanism

Interface mechanisms serve as transitional legal and economic buffers enabling the acquisition of resources from the existing market system without allowing market logic to re-enter the internal network. Physical resources not available in the network are crowdfunded from the emerging node community. This is a one-time buffer, not a permanent feature.

## Consequences

- The system is explicitly transitional. A fully non-monetary stage is embedded within but not imposed immediately.
- Competition is structurally eliminated — not regulated, not incentivised differently, absent from the technical design.
- The Integral Collective bootstrap can leverage the university anchor strategy for political feasibility in appropriate jurisdictions.
- Time credit as non-transferable ledger preserves reciprocity without creating extractable capital.
- AI-assisted design feedback enables self-regulation without centralised management.
